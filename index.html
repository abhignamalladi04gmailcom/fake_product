QR Code Integration Manufacturers integrate unique QR codes into their products during the production process. These codes contain essential information about the item, including its origin and authenticity. Step 2: Scan with QR Scanner Consumers, retailers, or inspectors use QR scanners (often available as mobile apps) to scan the code on the product's packaging or label. The scanner decodes the QR code to retrieve information.






What Are Fake Products? Fake products refer to counterfeit or imitation goods that are designed to mimic genuine products. These products are typically made to deceive consumers into believing they are purchasing the real thing. Types of Fake Products: Fake products can encompass a wide range of items, including counterfeit luxury goods (such as designer handbags or watches), counterfeit pharmaceuticals, fake electronics, and more. They can be found in various industries.






Ethical Considerations: Promoting or engaging in the production of fake products raises serious ethical questions. It erodes trust, damages businesses, and can harm consumers. Encouraging ethical behavior and transparency in the marketplace is crucial. Online Marketplaces: Online marketplaces face challenges in preventing the sale of fake products, as counterfeit goods can proliferate on these platforms. Some e-commerce sites have implemented anti-counterfeiting measures, but the issue persists.
